Why Study in Canada?

Why Study in Canada

A Canadian degree gives you more than an academic experience which helps you in your real-world experiences. Wherever you go in the world, a Canadian education opens the door of your career and practical life ahead.

  • Ranked universities: In you can find number of internationally ranked universities as compared to other destinations. It has a high number of ranked institutes such as University of Toronto, University of British Columbia, & McGill University. Many top universities are based in Canada.
  • Education quality: The standard of education in Canada has always been a center point attraction for international students. Various regulated bodies and independent teaming Canadian universities take a more practical approach rather than lecture-based learning for international students. Students are expected to take a proactive role and apply the skills in real-world scenarios. This allows deep understanding of your course which eventually benefits you in the long term.
  • Multicultural and secure: For international students, the diversity is reflected in every aspect of Canadian culture, which has ranked as a top multicultural and secure country for international students. The diversity is reflected in every aspect of Canadian culture, food, literature, arts, and music.
  • No language barrier: One of the biggest challenges for any international student is the local language of that country. In Canada, English and French are the official languages, and most courses are taught in English, making it easier for students from every part of the globe to study in Canada.

Average Tuition Fees in Canada for International Students

The locals pay a different fee as compared to international students planning to study in Canada, tuition fees vary depending on the university and course. The average tuition fees of undergraduate programs in Canada vary from $15,000 CAD - $40,000 CAD. The average tuition fees of postgraduate programs in Canada vary from $10,000 CAD - $35,000 CAD. There are various options for distance learning programs where online study programs are majorly available in Canada.

Working Hours Allowed in Canada (Student Visa)

As per Canadian regulations, as an international student you are allowed to work 20 hours/week during academic sessions and full-time during scheduled breaks. Working more than the allowed hours can strictly affect your visa status, resulting in cancellation of your study permit.

Average wage rates in Canada for international students are $15-$20 CAD/hour.

Living Cost in Canada for International Students

The average living cost for an international student in Canada depends on the city you are living in; the living cost in Toronto or Vancouver is different as compared to smaller cities.

  • $1,200 CAD per month for living in major cities like Toronto or Vancouver
  • $800 CAD per month for living in smaller cities

If you have a student loan or a government sponsorship, you will need to submit the evidence while submitting your visa application.
