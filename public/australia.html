Why Study in Australia?

Why Study in Australia

An Australian degree gives you more than an academic experience which helps you in your real-world experiences. Wherever you go in the world, an Australian education opens the door of your career and practical life ahead.

  • Ranked universities: In you can find number of internationally ranked universities as compared to other destinations. It has a high number of ranked institutes such as University of Sydney, University of Melbourne, & Australian National University. Many top universities are based in Australia.
  • Education quality: The standard of education in Australia has always been a center point attraction for international students. Various regulated bodies and independent teaming Australian universities take a more practical approach rather than lecture-based learning for international students. Students are expected to take a proactive role and apply the skills in real-world scenarios. This allows deep understanding of your course which eventually benefits you in the long term.
  • Multicultural and secure: For international students, the diversity is reflected in every aspect of Australian culture, which has ranked as a top multicultural and secure country for international students. The diversity is reflected in every aspect of Australian culture, food, literature, arts, and music.
  • No language barrier: One of the biggest challenges for any international student is the local language of that country. In Australia, English is the official language, and all courses are taught in English, making it easier for students from every part of the globe to study in Australia.

Average Tuition Fees in Australia for International Students

The locals pay a different fee as compared to international students planning to study in Australia, tuition fees vary depending on the university and course. The average tuition fees of undergraduate programs in Australia vary from $20,000 AUD - $45,000 AUD. The average tuition fees of postgraduate programs in Australia vary from $15,000 AUD - $40,000 AUD. There are various options for distance learning programs where online study programs are majorly available in Australia.

Working Hours Allowed in Australia (Student Visa)

As per Australian regulations, as an international student you are allowed to work 48 hours per fortnight during academic sessions and full-time during scheduled breaks. Working more than the allowed hours can strictly affect your visa status, resulting in cancellation of your study permit.

Average wage rates in Australia for international students are $20-$25 AUD/hour.

Living Cost in Australia for International Students

The average living cost for an international student in Australia depends on the city you are living in; the living cost in Sydney or Melbourne is different as compared to smaller cities.

  • $2,000 AUD per month for living in major cities like Sydney or Melbourne
  • $1,500 AUD per month for living in smaller cities

If you have a student loan or a government sponsorship, you will need to submit the evidence while submitting your visa application.
