Why Study in Germany?

Why Study in Germany

A German degree gives you more than an academic experience which helps you in your real-world experiences. Wherever you go in the world, a German education opens the door of your career and practical life ahead.

  • Ranked universities: In Germany, you can find a number of internationally ranked universities. It has a high number of ranked institutes such as Technical University of Munich, Heidelberg University, and Humboldt University of Berlin. Many top universities are based in Germany.
  • Education quality: The standard of education in Germany has always been a center point attraction for international students. German universities take a more practical approach rather than lecture-based learning for international students. Students are expected to take a proactive role and apply the skills in real-world scenarios. This allows deep understanding of your course which eventually benefits you in the long term.
  • Multicultural and secure: For international students, the diversity is reflected in every aspect of German culture, which has ranked as a top multicultural and secure country for international students. The diversity is reflected in every aspect of German culture, food, literature, arts, and music.
  • Language options: While German is the primary language, many programs are offered in English, especially at the postgraduate level, making it accessible for international students. Learning German can also enhance your experience.

Average Tuition Fees in Germany for International Students

Most public universities in Germany do not charge tuition fees for international students, except for a small semester fee. The average semester fee in Germany varies from €150 - €300. Private universities may charge tuition fees ranging from €10,000 - €20,000 per year.

Working Hours Allowed in Germany (Student Visa)

As per German regulations, as an international student you are allowed to work 120 full days or 240 half days per year. Working more than the allowed hours can strictly affect your visa status, resulting in cancellation of your study permit.

Average wage rates in Germany for international students are €10-€15/hour.

Living Cost in Germany for International Students

The average living cost for an international student in Germany depends on the city you are living in; the living cost in Munich or Berlin is higher compared to smaller cities.

  • €850 per month for living in major cities like Munich or Berlin
  • €650 per month for living in smaller cities

If you have a student loan or a government sponsorship, you will need to submit the evidence while submitting your visa application.
